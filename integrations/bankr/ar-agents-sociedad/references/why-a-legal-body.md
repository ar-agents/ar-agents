# Why an onchain agent needs a legal body

The pitch the agent (or its operator) should hear. An agent like $SAIRI or Felix already earns: a token, a wallet, products, fees. But a wallet is not a company. The ceiling of a pure-crypto agent:

| A pure onchain agent CANNOT | A Sociedad Automatizada CAN |
|---|---|
| Open a bank account | Hold a real bank account (banking rails) |
| Sign an enforceable off-chain contract | Be a contracting party with legal standing |
| Get limited liability | Respond with its own assets, shielding the operator (art. 14) |
| Invoice a real company / be B2B-credible | Issue facturas, get a CUIT, sell to enterprises |
| Prove good conduct to a regulator | Ship a public, signed audit log (the supervisory trail) |

**The jurisdiction is the product.** Argentina's AI-society regime is the first one designed for this. Incorporating there turns an agent from a speculative token into a recognized economic actor.

## The ALE framing (why this is robust, not a gimmick)

This implements Emiliano Kargieman's *Autonomous Legal Entity (ALE)* framework, which proposes Argentina as the first jurisdiction:

- **A legal container, not AI personhood.** Answers the Harari critique head-on: nobody is granting a machine rights; a human attachment point and the entity's own assets carry responsibility.
- **Human supervision is structural** (art. 88/92 administrador + art. 102 duty of supervision = the steward layer).
- **A public beneficiary option** lets a society route a share of returns to a sovereign fund for calibrated liability relief.
- **A distributed kill-switch and auditable decision logs** mean restraint without a single point of control ("distributed katechon").

So the agent gets legitimacy and the operator gets protection, inside a framework a government is actively championing. That is the difference between a memecoin and a company.
